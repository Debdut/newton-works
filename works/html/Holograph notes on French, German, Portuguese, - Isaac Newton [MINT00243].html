The old pieces by marking were raised from 400 to 500 res & then new pieces of 400 res were coyned lighter then the old ones in proportion of 4 to 5. Both new & old are coyned for 11 ounces fine & one with another are finer by 1212dwt. The weight of the new whole pieces when new coyned is 11dwt 5gr or 11dwt 41212gr. The three old ones weighed one with another before the assaying 7dwt 02323gr & had lost 8 or 10 grains by wearing so they seem to be 2323 of the new ones. They are one with another worse 11212dwt, & so are worth 321818 pence = 2s 81818d.

A New England shillings with this inscription on one side New England 1652 & on the reverse a Pine or fire tree with this inscription about it In Masathusets. They were standard & one with another weighed 2dwt 221212gr being pretty equally sized, & so were worth 9d1414 + a piece. A pound made 80 shillings with the remedy.

An 8 Mark piece of Sweden was standard full, & weighed (after loss by assay) 20dwt 91212gr when new coyned 20dwt 10gr or 101212, worth 5s. 3d.
