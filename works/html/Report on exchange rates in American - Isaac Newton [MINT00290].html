And we further lay before your Lordship that the Pillar pieces of 8 are better then the Sevil & Mexico by about 1d or 1d ob per piece, but considering that in the Colony of Massachusets Bay by vertue of an Act passed there & confirmed by the Royal authority, which cannot be altered but by an Act to be passed in the same manner, the Pillar pieces are current at the same rate with the Sevil & Mexico pieces,

And we further lay before yoer Lordships that pieces of eight of Sevil & Mexico of just weight & allay are worth 4s 6d per piece intrinsic value & that Pillar pieces of eight are finer silver & on that account more worth then the others of the same weight by three farthing in the piece: but considering that the difference is but small & that forreign coyns are already rated for the Colony of the Massachusets Bay by vertue of an Act there passed & confirmed by the Royal authority, which cannot be altered but by a new Act to be all forreign coynes are rated for thpassed in the same manner as their Lordships mention, the Pillar pieces of eight are there current at the same rate with the Sevil & Mexico pieces of eight: we are humbly of opinion that if her Majesty shall think fit to issue out her Royal Proclamation to the purpose proposed by their Lordships the coyn be rated for all her Majestys Plantations in Am. after the same manner as in this Act without making a difference between the Pillar pieces & the other pieces of eight.

Considering that most of her Majesties Plantations in Am. have already raised the coyns & that Pieces of 8 are raised from 4. 6 to 6 in New England by a law ratified by the Royal authority I humbly conceive it adviseable to limit the raising it further by her Majtys issuing such a Proclamation as the Lords Commissioners for Trade propose.



And considering the difficulties of reducing them to the Same rate as in England by reason that they are already raised in almost all tho her Majesties Dominions & in the colony of Massachusets Bay cannot be lowered without repealing an Act established by the Royal authority : we humbly conceive the rate proposed by their Lordships to be a convenient limit for putting a stop to the raising of forreign coyns beyond it, namely that [Sevil Mexico & Pillar Pieces of 8 should not pass for more then six shillings] such coyns do not pass at a higher rate

And considering the difficulties of reducing them to the same rate as in England by reason that they are already raised in almost all those Dominions & that in the colony of Massachusets bay they are raised by an Act established by the royal authority which cannot be repealed by by a like Act: we humbly conceive that their Lordships have fitly proposed the rate at which by the Said Act they pass in that Colony, as a limit above which they shall not pass in any other Colony.

May it please yoer Lordship



According to yoer Lordships order of Reference signified to us by Mr Lowndes his {illeg} of Dec 2d we ha{ve} considered the the Representation from the Lords Commissioners for the Council of Trade for setling the rates of Forreign coyns in het Majesties Plantations in America & humbly agree with their Lordships in their opinion that the altering the rates of the coynes at the discretion of those governments must incourage indirect practises of drawing the money from one Plantation to another to the undermining of each others trade & that the proper way to remedy this mischief is to reduce all forreign coynes to one & the same rate in all her Majesties Dominions in America.

We have therefore subjoined a Table to the just value of the principla forreign coyns in English money, that by the rate at which any one species shall pass it may be known at what rate all the other species are to pass. The rate proposed by their Lordships is that pieces of eight do not pass for more then six shillings, which is 1s 6d above their value in England, & for this limitation we rely upon their Lordships judgment it depending on the laws & customes of the Plantations & circumstances of Trade.

The Pillar Pieces of 8 are of finer silver then the rest & on that account more valuable by about three farthings in the piece besides some advantage in their weight. But considering that the difference is but small & is neglected in the Act passed in the Colony of the Massachusets Bay which appoints the rate of forreign coyns for that Province we are humbly of opinion that it may be neglected in rating the coyn for the rest of the Colonies, as in their Lordships Representation that Act being made the rule for rating the coyn for all her Majesties Plantations in America, because already ratified by the Royal authority.
